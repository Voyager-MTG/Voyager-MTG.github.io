### Retreat to the Borderholt
<change-preview>
https://github.com/Voyager-MTG/Voyager-MTG.github.io/blob/707cc6b310d3f9612172a89e12000b8e0f11ac7a/sets/WAW-files/img/32_Retreat%20to%20the%20Borderholt.jpg?raw=true
https://github.com/Voyager-MTG/Voyager-MTG.github.io/blob/4aed614d3395245a1ef9289e58430ff176990a02/sets/WAW-files/img/32_Retreat%20to%20the%20Borderholt.jpg?raw=true
</change-preview>

The root of the issue with borderholt, is a board wide type agnostic flicker. This effect is easily abused with enchantments or artifacts that’s designs assume there arent cards this efficient at flickering existing for balance. This results in large stacks and extraordinarily prolonged games as each card’s potential value is squeezed out of them. With the prevalence of permanent based removal in voyager, borderholt acts as a removal spell or wrath, effectively stabilizing against aggressive decks. Outside of that, retreat to borderholt is also a protection spell throwing a wrench into interaction, improving the currently strong enchantments deck both against aggressive and controlling matchups.

</left-card>

### Zoslera, Exiled Visionary
<change-preview>
https://github.com/Voyager-MTG/Voyager-MTG.github.io/blob/707cc6b310d3f9612172a89e12000b8e0f11ac7a/sets/FOE-files/img/329_Zoslera,%20Exiled%20Visionary.jpg?raw=true

</change-preview>

Zoslera is a powerful planeswalker that was made even stronger by becoming a growing draw engine that also grows on the removal mode. Moving both modes down a loyalty as well as forcing the player using Zoslera to get into combat makes the card more reasonable to deal with, and the counterplay is much more open for the opponent.

### Campsite
<change-preview>
https://github.com/Voyager-MTG/Voyager-MTG.github.io/blob/707cc6b310d3f9612172a89e12000b8e0f11ac7a/sets/FOE-files/img/330_Campsite.jpg?raw=true
https://github.com/Voyager-MTG/Voyager-MTG.github.io/blob/4aed614d3395245a1ef9289e58430ff176990a02/sets/FOE-files/img/330_Campsite.jpg?raw=true
</change-preview>

	Campsite is a card modeled after an interesting design originally made for Commander in Canon. It saw play in Pauper after a common reprint in Commander Masters, and seemed like it would be completely fine in terms of the play patterns it would present.

	This was completely and utterly wrong, because it turns out tutoring any card from the sanctum for the low low cost of 1 mana up front and 2 mana later is an incredibly inconsequential cost, especially considering the fact that you could play multiple copies, put the card you'd like away with the first one, and then save it from hand hate.

	This move brings it more in line with modern non-commander design sensibilities and allows for it to do its job, primarily as a tool to spend extra mana against aggro.

</left-card>
<right-card>
### Ignelscor Weaver
<change-preview>
https://github.com/Voyager-MTG/Voyager-MTG.github.io/blob/707cc6b310d3f9612172a89e12000b8e0f11ac7a/sets/FOE-files/img/298_Ignelscor%20Weaver.jpg?raw=true
https://github.com/Voyager-MTG/Voyager-MTG.github.io/blob/4aed614d3395245a1ef9289e58430ff176990a02/sets/FOE-files/img/298_Ignelscor%20Weaver.jpg?raw=true
</change-preview>

Ignelscor Weaver is a powerful midrange tool that pushes damage in a mostly healthy way, but the baseline body was pushing a bit too much damage as being a base 2/1 means that it can trade up again if needed. Moving it to a 1/1 will allow for the body to grow slowly while also providing far less on board presence.

</right-card>
<left-card>
### Ancient Viewdrifter
<change-preview>
https://github.com/Voyager-MTG/Voyager-MTG.github.io/blob/707cc6b310d3f9612172a89e12000b8e0f11ac7a/sets/WAW-files/img/228_Ancient%20Viewdrifter.jpg?raw=true
https://github.com/Voyager-MTG/Voyager-MTG.github.io/blob/4aed614d3395245a1ef9289e58430ff176990a02/sets/WAW-files/img/228_Ancient%20Viewdrifter.jpg?raw=true
</change-preview>

Ancient viewdrifter is a card that has very strong synergy points for a very low general cost to include. The ability to fix your mana while cantripping to trade in a typically low value body means that there is very little cost to include it in most decks, as it can still provide chip damage and has excellent keywords to use any number of power/toughness buffs on. It also asks opponents to play enough basics to avoid getting potentially stripmined very early in the game, which is not a major power concern, but does pose a future problem in deckbuilding.

</left-card>
